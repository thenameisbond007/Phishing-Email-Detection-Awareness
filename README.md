# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

## What is this project?

This is my Task 2 submission for the Future Interns Cybersecurity Internship.
The task was to look at real phishing emails, figure out what makes them dangerous,
and put together a proper report explaining everything in simple terms.

I honestly did not realise how sneaky phishing emails can be until I started
looking at them closely. Some of them are really well designed to trick people.

## What I did

- Collected 5 real email samples from public datasets and online sources
- Went through each one and looked for red flags like fake sender addresses,
  urgency language, suspicious links and weird greetings
- Classified each email as Safe, Suspicious or Phishing
- Wrote a full report explaining what I found and why it matters
- Added prevention tips that anyone can follow, not just tech people

## Things I learned

The biggest thing I learned is that phishing emails do not really need to be
technically advanced. They just need to make you panic. Once you know that,
they become a lot easier to spot.

The most common tricks I kept seeing were:
- Creating urgency like your account gets deleted in 24 hours
- Hiding who the real sender actually is
- Using generic greetings instead of your actual name
- Adding fake trust messages like this email has been scanned and is safe
