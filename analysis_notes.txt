EMAIL 3 — Email Security Upgrade Notice
================================================
Subject: Learn more about our updated email security and server maintenance
From: "Email Security" — no real company or domain shown

Red Flags Found:
- Sender name is just "Email Security" with no actual company behind it
- Greeting uses your email address instead of your real name
- Threatens that your account will be deleted in 7 days if you don't act
- Creates panic with a 24 hour deadline
- Has a big orange "Click here to verify" button which is a common phishing trap
- No company logo, no contact number, no real identity

Classification: PHISHING

Why I think this is Phishing:
This email is trying to scare you into clicking a button by threatening
to delete your account. No real email provider sends such vague threats
without showing who they are. The sender has hidden their real identity
and is using urgency and fear to trick you into clicking the verify button,
which would likely take you to a fake website to steal your login details.

Red Flags Found:
- Greeting is just the word "help," which makes no sense
- The subject line has a very long random ID number to look technical and official
- Threatens your password will expire in just 1 day to create panic
- Has a "Keep My Password" button which is designed to steal credentials
- Says "We won't be held responsible for any information lost" — no real company says this
- At the bottom it says "This mail has been scanned and considered safe" which is fake
- Signed by email@example.com which is a placeholder, not a real company

Classification: PHISHING

Why I think this is Phishing:
This email is full of manipulation tricks. The random ID number, the odd
greeting, the 1 day deadline, and the disclaimer at the bottom are all
designed to confuse and scare you. A real company managing your email
would address you by your actual name and would never say they are not
responsible for information loss. The "Keep My Password" button is a trap
to steal your login credentials.

EMAIL 5 — Messages Delivery Failure
================================================
Subject: Notice: Undelivered Mail Returned to Sender
From: example.com

Red Flags Found:
- Sender domain is just "example.com" which is too generic to be real
- Claims 7 messages are stuck but gives no details about what messages
- The "Review" and "Delete" links have no real destination shown
- Signed by "Mail Administrator" with no actual name or company
- The exact timestamp given looks artificially added to seem official

Classification: SUSPICIOUS

Why I think this is Suspicious:
This email looks like it could be either a real delivery failure notice
or a fake one. Real mail servers do send bounce back messages sometimes,
but a genuine one would clearly show your email provider name and give
more specific details about which emails failed. Because the sender is
too vague and the links have no clear destination, I cannot confirm it
is safe. I would not click any links in this email and would check
directly by logging into my email account instead.
